1   package org.infinispan.atomic;
2   
3   import java.util.Map;
4   
5   import org.infinispan.atomic.impl.AtomicHashMap;
6   import org.infinispan.util.concurrent.IsolationLevel;
7   
8   /**
9    * FineGrainedAtomicMap is a special type of Map geared for use in Infinispan.  FineGrainedAtomicMap has 
10   * two major characteristics:
11   *
12   * <ol>
13   *    <li>Atomic locking and isolation is applied on keys of FineGrainedAtomicMap rather than entire map itself</li>
14   *    <li>Fine-grained serialization of deltas</li>
15   * </ol>
16   *
17   * <b><u>1.  Fine-grained atomic locking and isolation</u></b>
18   * <p>
19   * FineGrainedAtomicMap allows fine grained locking of entries within the map; it also isolates the map for safe 
20   * reading (see {@link IsolationLevel} while concurrent writes may be going on.
21   * </p>
22   * <br />
23   * <b><u>2.  Fine-grained serialization of deltas</u></b>
24   * <p>
25   * AtomicMap implementations also implement the {@link DeltaAware} interface.  This powerful interface allows the
26   * generation and application of deltas, and requires that implementations are capable of tracking changes made to it
27   * during the course of a transaction.  This helps since when performing replications to update remote nodes, the
28   * <i>entire</i> map need not be serialized and transported all the time, as serializing and transporting {@link Delta}
29   * instances would work just as well, and typically be much smaller and hence faster to serialize and transport.
30   * </p>
31   * <br />
32   * <br />
33   * <b><u>Usage</u></b>
34   * <p>
35   * FineGrainedAtomicMap should be constructed and "registered" with Infinispan using the {@link AtomicMapLookup} helper.  This
36   * helper ensures thread safe construction and registration of AtomicMap instances in Infinispan's data container.  E.g.:
37   * <br />
38   * <code>
39   *    FineGrainedAtomicMap&lt;String, Integer&gt; map = AtomicMapLookup.getFineGrainedAtomicMap(cache, "my_atomic_map_key");
40   * </code>
41   * </p>
42   * <p><b><u>Referential Integrity</u></b><br />
43   * It is important to note that concurrent readers of an AtomicMap will essentially have the same view of the contents
44   * of the underlying structure, but since AtomicMaps use internal proxies, readers are isolated from concurrent writes
45   * and {@link IsolationLevel#READ_COMMITTED} and {@link IsolationLevel#REPEATABLE_READ} semantics are guaranteed.
46   * However, this guarantee is only present if the values stored in an AtomicMap are <i>immutable</i> (e.g., Strings,
47   * primitives, and other immutable types).</p>
48   *
49   * <p>Mutable value objects which happen to be stored in an AtomicMap may be updated and, prior to being committed,
50   * or even replaced in the map, be visible to concurrent readers.  Hence, AtomicMaps are <b><i>not suitable</i></b> for
51   * use with mutable value objects.</p>
52   * </p>
53   * <br />
54   * <p>
55   * This interface, for all practical purposes, is just a marker interface that indicates that maps of this type will
56   * be locked atomically in the cache and replicated in a fine grained manner, as it does not add any additional methods
57   * to {@link java.util.Map}.
58   * </p>
59   *
60   * @author Vladimir Blagojevic
61   * @see DeltaAware
62   * @see Delta
63   * @see AtomicHashMap
64   * @see AtomicMapLookup
65   * @since 5.1
66   */
67  public interface FineGrainedAtomicMap<K, V> extends Map<K, V> {
68  }
